Editorial Policy - The Crippling Truth

The Crippling Truth is a satirical news and commentary platform dedicated to highlighting issues surrounding disability rights, social care, government policy, and broader societal attitudes toward disabled people in the UK. Our mission is to inform, challenge, and entertain through sharp, factual satire that exposes injustices while giving a voice to disabled communities.

We believe that humour can be a powerful tool for social commentary, provided it remains grounded in truth and respects the lived experiences of disabled people.

The Crippling Truth maintains complete editorial independence. Our editorial decisions are free from influence by advertisers, sponsors, political parties, or external stakeholders. All content decisions are made solely by our editorial team to ensure integrity, impartiality, and transparency.

  • Rigorous Research: Verified claims, statistics, and references from credible sources.
  • Clear Distinction: Between factual reporting, opinion, and satire.
  • Corrections Policy: Prompt updates with transparent corrections if inaccuracies occur.
  • Original Content: All content is original and not republished without explicit permission.
  • Transparency: Clear bylines, publication dates, and author information included.
  • Respectful Satire: Satire targets systems and societal attitudes—not individuals.
  • Minor Corrections: Noted with an “Updated” timestamp.
  • Major Corrections: Clearly stated with an explanation.
  • Reader Feedback: Reports can be made via our Contact Us page.

We credit all external sources. Third-party content is clearly attributed, with links where possible.

The editorial direction is overseen by our Editor-in-Chief and a diverse board with lived experience of disability.

We welcome submissions from disabled writers and activists. Submissions undergo editorial review for quality and relevance.

  • Respect for Privacy: Personal information is never published without consent.
  • Sensitivity in Reporting: We avoid perpetuating harmful stereotypes.
  • Accountability: We hold ourselves and those in power accountable.
